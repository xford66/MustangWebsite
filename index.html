The Story

Born to Run

The 1969 Ford Mustang Fastback arrived at a pivotal moment in American muscle car history. Ford's designers, led by Larry Shinoda, gave the Mustang a longer, lower, wider stance — and the iconic SportsRoof fastback roofline that swept dramatically to the rear.

It wasn't just beautiful — it was built for the 1969 racing season. The Boss 302 variant was created specifically to homologate for the Trans-Am championship. The Boss 429 was designed to get NASCAR-legal engines on the street.

"It's not just a car. It's a statement about who you are and where you're going."

Today, the '69 Fastback is one of the most recognizable and sought-after muscle cars in the world — immortalized by films, races, and decades of passion from collectors worldwide.

Power

Under the Hood

250 Six
155 HP · Inline-6

The entry-level option. Dependable and economical — a Mustang for everyday driving without sacrificing the style.

302 V8
220 HP · Small Block

The sweet spot. Spirited performance with manageable power, this is what most '69 Mustang owners drove off the lot.

Boss 302
290 HP · High-Rev V8

Built for the Trans-Am series. Revved to 7,000 RPM and screamed like nothing else on the road. A true driver's machine.

428 CJ
335 HP · Cobra Jet

The Cobra Jet was Ford's drag-strip weapon. Massive torque from idle, able to devour quarter miles in the low 13-second range.

Boss 429
375 HP · NASCAR V8

An engine so large it barely fit the engine bay. Hand-assembled, NASCAR-derived, and utterly brutal. The rarest '69 of all.
